Hindi Sentiment Analysis System

Sentiment Analysis (SA) is a natural language processing task that uses the computational approach to extract the opinionated content and classifies it as positive, negative or neutral. Generally, sentiment analysis can be performed at three levels, i.e., document, sentence and aspect level.


Sentence Level analysis

It determines whether each sentence expressed a positive, negative, or neutral opinion.


Document Level analysis

Document level considers that a document is an opinion on an entity or aspect of it. The task is to classify whether a whole opinion document expresses a positive or negative sentiment. For example, given a product review, the system determines whether the review expresses an overall positive or negative opinion about the product.

Aspect Level Ananlysis

Aspect-based sentiment analysis helps to capture nuances about the objects of interest. As sometimes sentences may consist of different sentiments associated to different aspects of an entity and overall polarity of the sentence does not help to identify the absolute opinion of the writer. For example, consider the sentence, “The story of the movie is weak but music is good”. This sentence consists of negative (i.e., “weak”) and positive (i.e., “good”) sentiments about aspects “story” and “music”, respectively.
